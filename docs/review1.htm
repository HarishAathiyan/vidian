HOW GOOD IS THE NEW ESP32-C3? - vidian

A simple product overview and comparison with other popular boards

Expressif systems recently talked about the specs and released the datasheet of the new SOC microcontroller, the ESP32-C3 which can be expected to be released soon in the market next to the popular ESP8266 and ESP32 microcontrollers which our IOT makers mostly prefer for their projects. It will be a perfect replacement for ESP8266 if you are still sticking it around in 2021.

What makes it unique from normal microcontrollers?

First of all its a RISC-V (say risc five) which is a popular architecture for reduced instructions set computing. It reaches up to 160 MHz maximum which makes it unique compared to the slow 16 MHz microcontrollers on the market. And while comparing the speeds of ESP32 with the ESP32-C3, The ESP32-C3 is considerably slow at frequency.

DEVKITS FOR ESP32-C3:

There are currently some sample devkits in the expressif website to buy from. They come with an integrated serial to USB converters for communication with the computer. It also has a voltage regulator for regulating the incoming voltage from 5v to 3.3v. It has an on-board boot and reset button. They come in a 30 or 32 pin type. They both two have an on-board antenna for Wi-Fi and Bluetooth.

DIFFERENT VARIENTS OF ESP32-C3:

The ESP32-C3 has two variants for portability and extra storage. They are the ESP32-C3-WROOM-1 and the ESP32-C3-MINI-1.

The ESP32-C3-MINI-1 is considerably smaller than the WROOM versions form factor.

Here is the two devkits with the WROOM and MINI variant where the MINI version is smaller.

The esp32-C3 comes with a handful of security features.

FINAL VERDICT:

The ESP32-C3 is the SOC microcontroller that is good enough for your IOT projects that full fills most of your requirements. It has a RISC-V core at 160 MHz which is powerful enough to render a graphics for your tft display at near to Real time. It has a large memory to store more data. It has WIFI and Bluetooth embedded on it so no need to waste your pins for sticking external Wi-Fi and Bluetooth modules. It has nice set of peripherals to stick most of your sensors and displays you wish to research.
